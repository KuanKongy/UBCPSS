What is Project STEM Search?

Your launchpad into undergraduate research

As finding research is often a daunting journey, Project STEM Search supports students by bridging the gap between classroom learning and hands-on research.

Our supportive interdisciplinary community ensures that students build confidence and prepare for a successful career in research and beyond.

🔬

Unique research experience

Personalized guidance to help you land real research positions across all STEM fields.

Who can join Project STEM Search?+

Any UBC undergraduate student — no prior research experience required. We're built for all stages, from first-years exploring to upper-years ready to apply.

Is there a membership fee?+

A small AMS club fee applies when you sign up. The vast majority of our events are free for all members once you've joined.

What STEM fields does PSS cover?+

All of them — biology, chemistry, CS, physics, engineering, neuroscience, psychology, statistics, and more. We're interdisciplinary by design.

How often do events happen?+

Roughly 2–3 events per month throughout the academic year (September through April), mixing professor panels, workshops, and socials.

How do I actually find a research position?+

Through professor spotlight events, resume and cold-email workshops, and mentorship from upper-year members who've been through the process.

Do I need to attend every event?+

Not at all. Come to whatever fits your schedule. Just RSVP through our Linktree so we can plan capacity for each event.
